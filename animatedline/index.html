Urban CO2 Emissions
Mobile Emissions Mapping: Currently, the tools don't exist to monitor and map Greenhouse Gases (GHGs) at the local to city scale. At UBC, we're developing sensors that can be attached to vehicles and/bikes to map GHG at policy relevant scales.

A Mobile CO2 Monitoring Platform

The global average CO2 mixing ratio is 397.8 ppm. This means that globally, this is the baseline of CO2 in the atmosphere. Any measured value above this level is CO2 that is put into the atmosphere through fossil fuel combustion from heating, ventilation, and cooling systems, transportation, industrial processes, human respiration, and to a lesser degree plant respiration.

Research by the Urban Climate Group at University of British Columbia is currently focused on trying to understand the complex interplay between the social and physical dimensions of cities that result in their increased emissions. They are particularly interested to map the spatial and temporal dimensions of emissions so that cities - their governments and residents - can begin to find ways to reduce emissions and build more sustainable and liveable cities.

Reducing GHG emissions will be critical for mitigating global climate uncertainty. When looking at the measured emissions a long the various routes, notice where emissions might be high or low.
