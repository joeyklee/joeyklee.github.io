9th International Conference on Urban Climates

This talk is about a pilot study:

  • 5 mobile CO2 sensors - deployed in a focused measurement campaign
  • Evaluation of relationship between observed mixing ratios and traffic and building emissions inventories.
  • Mapping CO2 emissions derived from the observed mixing ratios using the Trace Gas Flux Density model.

Context

The Question:

Can we map greenhouse gases (GHG) and other pollutants, specifically CO2, at high spatio-temporal resolution across the city with a network of mobile sensors?

System Specifications

  • Runs on 12 V Power supply
  • Measures CO2 mixing ratios (ppm), Geoposition, and Air temperature at 1-second intervals
  • Size: 35.8 cm x 27.8 cm x 11.8 cm, weight: 2.6 kg.
  • Precision: +/- 1.1 ppm
  • Accuracy: +/- 3ppm, with maximum drift of +/- 4 ppm over seven days.
  • Total delayed response time: 13 s with 3 m sample tube at flow rate of 700cc/min.

Limitations

  • Strict meteorological requirements: needs weak horizontal advection and strong vertical mixing!
  • Calculating CO2 emissions requires intstrumentation (e.g. flux tower).
  • Sampling Density: is 3 km2h-1 enough?

Conclusions & Future Directions
