Research Question:

Can we map greenhouse gases (GHG) and other pollutants, specifically CO2, at high spatio-temporal resolution across the city with a network of mobile sensors?

System Specifications

  • Runs on 12 V Power supply
  • Measures CO2 mixing ratios (ppm), geoposition and speed, and air temperature at 1-second intervals
  • Size: 35.8 cm x 27.8 cm x 11.8 cm, weight: 2.6 kg.
  • Precision: +/- 1.1 ppm
  • Accuracy: +/- 3 ppm, with maximum drift of +/- 4 ppm over seven days.
  • Total delayed response time: 13 s with 3 m sample tube at flow rate of 700 cc/min.

Conclusions & Future Directions

  • Mobile CO2 sensing shows potential to map emissions.
  • Potential applications for pollutant mapping.
  • Visualization & feedback opportunities in progress.
  • Collaboration potential with local mobility providers for longterm & spatially extensive/intensive monitoring.

Recap

  • A Mobile CO2 sensing system was developed using opensource components.
  • The system was tested in Vancouver and evaluated against traffic and building emissions inventories.
  • The study shows the potential for pervasive mobile sensing in GHG and pollutant monitoring in cities.
  • Future opportunities exist for long term monitoring and to provide feddback through visualization.
